The Churn Rate Conundrum: How to Calculate SaaS Runway When Your Churn Rate is Unpredictable

You're a bootstrapped SaaS founder, and you're constantly juggling multiple plates to keep your business afloat. One of the most critical metrics you need to keep an eye on is your runway – the amount of time your business can sustain itself before it runs out of cash. But, what happens when your churn rate is unpredictable? In this article, we'll explore the mistake founders make when ignoring churn in runway calculation and provide a step-by-step guide on how to calculate SaaS runway with an unpredictable churn rate.

The Problem with Ignoring Churn

When calculating your runway, most founders focus on their monthly recurring revenue (MRR) and customer acquisition cost (CAC). However, they often ignore one crucial factor: churn rate. Churn rate is the percentage of customers who cancel their subscription per month. Ignoring churn can lead to a significant underestimation of your runway, leaving you with a false sense of security.

The Math Behind Churn Rate

Churn rate is typically calculated as:

Churn Rate = (Customers Lost / Customers at Start) x 100

However, when your churn rate is unpredictable, this calculation becomes more complex. You need to consider the compounding effect of churn over time. The annual churn rate is calculated as:

Annual Churn Rate = 1 - (1 - Monthly Churn Rate)^12

For example, if your monthly churn rate is 5%, your annual churn rate would be 46%.

Calculating SaaS Runway with Unpredictable Churn Rate

To calculate your SaaS runway with an unpredictable churn rate, you need to consider the following factors:

  1. MRR
  2. CAC
  3. Churn rate (both monthly and annual)
  4. Customer lifetime value (CLV)

Here's a step-by-step guide to calculate your SaaS runway:

  1. Determine your MRR and CAC.
  2. Calculate your monthly churn rate and annual churn rate.
  3. Calculate your CLV using the following formula:

CLV = (MRR / Churn Rate) x 12

  1. Calculate your runway using the following formula:

Runway = (CLV / CAC) x 12

The Numbers Behind This

Let's assume your MRR is $2,000, CAC is $100, monthly churn rate is 5%, and annual churn rate is 46%. Using the formulas above, we get:

CLV = ($2,000 / 0.05) x 12 = $48,000 Runway = ($48,000 / $100) x 12 = 5.76 months

What To Do Right Now

Calculating your SaaS runway with an unpredictable churn rate requires a more nuanced approach than simply ignoring churn. By considering the compounding effect of churn over time, you can get a more accurate picture of your business's financial health. Take the following steps:

  1. Track your churn rate regularly to get a better understanding of your business's churn dynamics.
  2. Use the formulas above to calculate your CLV and runway.
  3. Adjust your pricing strategy and customer acquisition strategy to mitigate the impact of churn.

By following these steps, you can ensure that your SaaS business has a sustainable runway, even with an unpredictable churn rate.
