The Churn Rate Trap: How to Calculate SaaS Runway When Your Churn Rate is Unpredictable

As a bootstrapped SaaS founder, you're no stranger to the stress of managing cash flow. But have you ever found yourself staring at a spreadsheet, wondering how long your runway will last, only to realize that your churn rate is making it impossible to predict? You're not alone.

The Problem with Ignoring Churn in Runway Calculation

When calculating your SaaS runway, it's easy to focus on the number of customers you have, your average revenue per user (ARPU), and your customer acquisition cost (CAC). But if you're ignoring your churn rate, you're making a critical mistake. Churn rate is the percentage of customers who cancel their subscriptions each month, and it can have a devastating impact on your runway.

The Reality of Churn Rate

Let's say you have 1,000 customers, with an ARPU of $29 and a CAC of $100. If your churn rate is 5%, you might think you're in good shape. But the reality is that 50 customers will cancel each month, leaving you with a significantly reduced revenue stream. And if you're not accounting for this in your runway calculation, you're setting yourself up for disaster.

The Compounding Effect of Churn

Churn rate is not just a one-time event; it's a compounding effect that can quickly eat away at your revenue. If you're losing 5% of your customers each month, you'll lose 60% of your customers in a year. That's not just a problem for your cash flow; it's a problem for your business's very survival.

How to Calculate SaaS Runway with Churn Rate

So how do you calculate your SaaS runway when your churn rate is unpredictable? The answer is to use a more nuanced approach that takes into account the compounding effect of churn. Here's a simple formula to get you started:

Runway = (MRR x 12) / (Churn Rate x 12 + 1)

Where:

  • MRR is your monthly recurring revenue
  • Churn Rate is your monthly churn rate as a decimal
  • 12 is the number of months in a year

The Numbers Behind This

Let's say you have a MRR of $2,900 and a churn rate of 5%. Plugging these numbers into the formula, you get:

Runway = ($2,900 x 12) / (0.05 x 12 + 1) Runway = $34,800 / 1.6 Runway = 21.75 months

This means that if you don't change your business strategy, you'll run out of cash in approximately 21.75 months.

What To Do Right Now

If you're ignoring your churn rate in your runway calculation, it's time to take action. Here are a few steps you can take right now:

  1. Track your churn rate: Start tracking your churn rate to get a better understanding of how it's impacting your business.
  2. Analyze your data: Use your data to identify the reasons behind your churn rate and make changes to your business strategy accordingly.
  3. Develop a retention strategy: Focus on developing a retention strategy that will help you keep your customers and reduce your churn rate.

By taking these steps, you can get a better handle on your churn rate and make more accurate predictions about your SaaS runway.
