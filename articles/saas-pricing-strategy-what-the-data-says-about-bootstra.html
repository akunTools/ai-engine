SaaS Pricing Strategy: What the Data Says About Bootstrapped Products Under $100/month

Bootstrapped SaaS founders often struggle with pricing their products. With limited resources and a focus on growth, it's tempting to adopt a low-cost, high-volume approach. However, this strategy can lead to undervaluing your product and sacrificing revenue. In this article, we'll explore the data behind SaaS pricing strategies for bootstrapped products under $100/month.

The Problem with Low-Cost Pricing

When pricing your SaaS product too low, you risk undervaluing your solution and creating a perception of low quality. This can lead to:

  • Difficulty scaling your business, as customers may not be willing to pay more for your product
  • Increased churn rates, as customers may be more likely to cancel their subscriptions if they feel they're not getting value for their money
  • Reduced revenue, as you're not maximizing the potential value of your product

The Data on SaaS Pricing

According to ProfitWell research, the median ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) for bootstrapped B2B SaaS products is between $40-80 per month. However, this number can vary widely depending on factors such as industry, target market, and product complexity.

Here are some real-world pricing benchmarks for SaaS products under $100/month:

  • B2B SaaS (SMB): $19-49 per month ( median ARPU: $29)
  • B2B SaaS (Enterprise): $49-99 per month (median ARPU: $74)
  • B2C SaaS: $9-29 per month (median ARPU: $19)

Pricing Strategies for Bootstrapped SaaS

When developing a pricing strategy for your bootstrapped SaaS product, consider the following:

  • Value-based pricing: Price your product based on the value it provides to customers, rather than its cost or competition.
  • Tiered pricing: Offer multiple pricing tiers to accommodate different customer needs and budgets.
  • Freemium pricing: Offer a free version of your product to attract new customers and encourage upgrades to paid plans.

The Numbers Behind This

Let's consider an example of a bootstrapped SaaS product with a pricing strategy based on value. Suppose your product, a project management tool, provides significant value to customers by increasing their productivity and reducing their workload.

  • Pricing: $49 per month ( median ARPU: $49)
  • Customer acquisition cost: $10 per customer (CAC)
  • Customer lifetime value: $1,000 (CLV)
  • Churn rate: 2% per month (healthy range for early-stage SaaS)

In this scenario, your revenue would be $49 per month per customer, with a total revenue of $588 per month. Your customer acquisition cost would be $10 per customer, and your customer lifetime value would be $1,000.

What To Do Right Now

To develop a successful pricing strategy for your bootstrapped SaaS product, follow these steps:

  1. Conduct market research: Analyze your competition and target market to determine the optimal pricing for your product.
  2. Value your product: Determine the value your product provides to customers and price it accordingly.
  3. Test and iterate: Test your pricing strategy and iterate based on customer feedback and revenue data.

By following these steps and using real-world data, you can develop a pricing strategy that maximizes revenue and growth for your bootstrapped SaaS product.
