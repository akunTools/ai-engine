The SaaS Runway Calculator Mistake: Ignoring Unpredictable Churn Rates

As a bootstrapped SaaS founder, you're no stranger to the importance of cash flow. But have you ever found yourself caught off guard by a sudden spike in churn rate? If so, you're not alone. Many founders make the critical mistake of ignoring unpredictable churn rates when calculating their SaaS runway.

The Problem: Ignoring Churn in Runway Calculation

When calculating your SaaS runway, it's easy to get caught up in the numbers. You focus on your monthly recurring revenue (MRR), customer acquisition cost (CAC), and growth rate. But what about churn? If your churn rate is unpredictable, you're setting yourself up for a financial shock.

Let's say your MRR is $10,000 per month, and your CAC is $1,000. You're growing at a rate of 10% per month, and you're confident that your churn rate is around 5%. Sounds good, right? But what if your churn rate suddenly spikes to 15%? Your runway just got a lot shorter.

The Solution: Accounting for Unpredictable Churn Rates

So, how do you account for unpredictable churn rates in your SaaS runway calculation? The answer lies in using a more conservative estimate of your churn rate. This will give you a more accurate picture of your runway and help you prepare for the worst-case scenario.

Here's an example:

  • MRR: $10,000 per month
  • CAC: $1,000
  • Growth rate: 10% per month
  • Churn rate: 10% (conservative estimate)

Using a SaaS runway calculator, you can plug in these numbers and get an estimate of your runway. But what if your actual churn rate is higher? You'll need to adjust your estimate accordingly.

The Numbers Behind This

Let's say your actual churn rate is 15%, and you don't adjust your estimate. Your runway would be significantly shorter than you thought. In fact, your runway would be reduced by 50% compared to the conservative estimate.

| Churn Rate | Runway (months) | | --- | --- | | 5% | 12 months | | 10% | 6 months | | 15% | 3 months |

What To Do Right Now

If you're a bootstrapped SaaS founder, it's essential to account for unpredictable churn rates in your SaaS runway calculation. Here's what you can do right now:

  1. Review your churn rate history and identify any patterns or trends.
  2. Use a conservative estimate of your churn rate to calculate your runway.
  3. Adjust your estimate regularly to reflect changes in your churn rate.
  4. Consider using a SaaS runway calculator with churn rate to get a more accurate estimate of your runway.

By following these steps, you'll be better prepared for the unexpected and can build a more sustainable business.
