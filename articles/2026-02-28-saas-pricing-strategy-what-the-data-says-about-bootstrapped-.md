## The State of SaaS Pricing

According to recent data, the median ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) for bootstrapped B2B SaaS products is between $40-80 per month. This range is not surprising, given the competitive nature of the market. To stand out, you need to offer value that justifies the price point.

## Pricing Tiers and Their Impact

Research suggests that most successful SaaS products use pricing tiers, offering different levels of features and support. This approach allows you to cater to a wide range of customers, from individual users to large enterprises. By structuring your pricing tiers, you can:

* Increase average revenue per user (ARPU)
* Improve customer satisfaction and retention
* Enhance your brand's perceived value

## Pricing Benchmarks for Bootstrapped SaaS

When it comes to pricing benchmarks, here are some key takeaways:

* Most common price points: $19, $29, $49, $99 per month
* Annual discount: 15-20% is standard (2 months free = 17%)
* Churn rate: 2-5% per month is common for B2B SaaS (SMB)

## The Numbers Behind This

Let's consider an example. Suppose you're offering a SaaS product with a monthly price of $49. With an annual discount of 17%, your customers would pay $41.33 per month. If you have 100 customers, your MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) would be $4,133. This is a significant revenue stream, especially considering the low churn rate.

## What To Do Right Now

To implement a data-driven SaaS pricing strategy, follow these steps:

1. **Analyze your customer base**: Understand your customers' needs, pain points, and willingness to pay.
2. **Research your competition**: Identify pricing tiers, features, and support offered by similar products.
3. **Set clear goals**: Determine your revenue targets, growth rate, and customer acquisition costs.
4. **Test and iterate**: Experiment with different pricing tiers, features, and support levels to find the sweet spot.
