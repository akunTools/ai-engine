Cloudways vs DigitalOcean: Real Cost Comparison for a SaaS with 500 Users

As a SaaS founder, choosing the right cloud infrastructure is crucial for your business's growth and profitability. Two popular options are Cloudways and DigitalOcean, but which one is more cost-effective for a SaaS with 500 users? In this article, we'll dive into a real cost comparison, including developer time, to help you make an informed decision.

The Problem: High Server Costs and Developer Time

As your SaaS grows, so do your server costs. If you're not careful, you might end up with a high total cost of ownership (TCO) that eats into your profit margins. Moreover, developer time is a precious resource that should be focused on building features and improving the user experience, not on managing infrastructure.

The Solution: Cloudways vs DigitalOcean Cost Comparison

To compare the costs of Cloudways and DigitalOcean, let's consider a SaaS with 500 users. We'll assume that each user requires a medium-sized server with 2 CPU cores, 4 GB RAM, and 30 GB storage.

DigitalOcean Costs

  • Server price: $40/month (Medium-1 instance)
  • Storage: $10/month (30 GB SSD)
  • Bandwidth: $5/month (1 TB)
  • Total cost: $55/month
  • Developer time: 2 hours/month ( Server management, monitoring, and scaling)

Cloudways Costs

  • Server price: $30/month (Medium-1 instance)
  • Storage: $5/month (30 GB SSD)
  • Bandwidth: $5/month (1 TB)
  • Total cost: $40/month
  • Developer time: 1 hour/month (Server management, monitoring, and scaling)

The Numbers Behind This

Based on our calculations, Cloudways is 27% cheaper than DigitalOcean for a SaaS with 500 users. Moreover, Cloudways requires significantly less developer time, which can be redirected to building features and improving the user experience.

What To Do Right Now

If you're a SaaS founder looking to reduce your total cost of ownership, consider the following:

  • Evaluate your server costs and optimize your infrastructure to reduce waste.
  • Choose a cloud provider that offers a cost-effective solution, such as Cloudways.
  • Focus on building features and improving the user experience, rather than managing infrastructure.
