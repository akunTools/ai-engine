The Engineering Behind the Metrics

20 years of web development experience distilled into ruthless, mathematical truths.

The Journey

I wrote my first line of production code two decades ago. Since then, I've watched the web evolve from static HTML pages into massive, complex SaaS ecosystems. I've witnessed the dot-com aftermath, the Web 2.0 craze, and the endless "growth-at-all-costs" era heavily funded by Venture Capitalists.

Through it all, one truth remains: the most resilient businesses aren't the ones burning millions on vanity PR. They are the bootstrapped SaaS companies. They are built by founders and engineers who obsess over unit economics, server infrastructure, and actual profitability.

Why I Built SaaS Tools

Most SaaS advice online is written for venture-backed startups with infinite runways. When you are bootstrapping, that advice is dangerous. Every dollar, every unmanaged server crash, and every churned customer directly impacts your livelihood.

I built this platform to provide clear, utilitarian calculators and guides to help founders make financial decisions without the guesswork. The tools here are not generic marketing widgets—they are battle-tested engineering frameworks designed to give you the brutal, honest numbers about your break-even points, burn rate, and scaling costs.

Our Alignment with You

As detailed in our Privacy Policy, this site is built with a strict Privacy-First philosophy. Having been a developer for 20 years, I despise invasive surveillance tracking. That’s why SaaS Tools uses no tracking cookies, requires no logins, and doesn't sell your data.

To keep the platform free, independent, and ad-free, we partner exclusively with infrastructure providers we trust (like Cloudways). If you upgrade your servers through our links, it helps keep these calculators running—but it will never influence the hard math our tools output.
